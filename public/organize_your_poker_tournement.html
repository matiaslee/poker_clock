Poker Tournament Rules

üÉè Prepare the Chips

  • Make sure you have enough chips for:
    • Initial buy-ins (2000 chips per player)
    • Rebuys (2000 chips each)
    • Add-ons (3000 chips per player)
  • Use clearly distinguishable chip denominations.

üé≤ Buy-in

  • Determine the initial buy-in amount (e.g., $10).
  • Each player receives 2,000 chips with their buy-in.

üíµ Rebuys

  • Rebuys are allowed during the first hour only.
  • Players can rebuy if:
    • They go bust (have 0 chips), or
    • Their chip stack is below a threshold (e.g., under 500 chips).
  • Cost of each rebuy is the same as initial buy-in.

‚ûï Add-ons

  • One-time add-on available at the end of the first hour (usually during the first break).
  • Each player can add 3,000 chips, regardless of their stack.
  • Add-on cost = same as or higher than the buy-in (your choice).

üïê Tournament Clock

  • Use our clock to track blind levels and breaks.
    • Levels: e.g., 10-minute blinds increasing gradually.
    • Break after level 6 (~1 hour) for add-ons.
  • Pause the clock for breaks and chip counts.

üí∞ Payouts

  • Decide how the prize pool will be distributed. Popular formats:
    • Top 3 players (e.g., 50% / 30% / 20%)
    • Winner takes all
  • Include rebuy and add-on money in the total prize pool.
  • If you plan to play regularly, consider organizing an annual tournament and setting aside part of the money to purchase a special prize for the finale.

üçª Extras

  • Provide snacks, drinks, and a great atmosphere!
  • Take photos of the final table or the winner!
